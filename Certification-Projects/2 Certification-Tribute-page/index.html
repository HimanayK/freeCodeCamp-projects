Alfred Nobel’s life and work:

  • Alfred Nobel was born in Sweden, grew up in Russia, studied chemistry and technology in France and the US
  • St. Petersburg, 1842-1863 - The young Alfred grew up, received his education and was shaped as a human being in the cosmopolitan atmosphere of St. Petersburg
  • At forty years old, Alfred Nobel bought an elegant mansion in Avenue Malakoff, a fashionable quarter near the Arc de Triomphe and Bois-de-Boulogne
  • Dynamite: A Game-Changing Invention - Nobel invented dynamite in 1867, a safer and more manageable form of nitroglycerin, which revolutionized construction, mining, and warfare
  • Nobel’s Philanthropic Endeavor -
  • Alfred Nobel’s life is a testament to the power of innovation, global vision, and philanthropy. His journey from being a dynamite entrepreneur to a beacon of global recognition in various fields serves as an inspiration for entrepreneurs and leaders worldwide.
  • Toward the end of his life, he wrote the tragedy Nemesis. His best literary form of expression was probably the aphorism, where he often expressed himself drastically.

    “Hope is nature’s veil for hiding truth’s nakedness.”

    “Lying is the greatest of all sins.”

    “Self-respect without the respect of others is like a jewel which will not stand the daylight.”
